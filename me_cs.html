Me as a computer scientist

I am a German student and would like to become a computer scientist

I have been engaged with computers throughout my entire life. My journey commenced at the age of six with Paint. Eventually, I received a fairly powerful laptop from my father, allowing me to delve into the realm of Minecraft.

In addition to gaming, I have always harbored a keen interest in video editing and image manipulation, using tools like Shotcut and paint.net in the past. During a school project week, I even ventured into crafting a rudimentary website, back then solely employing HTML.

My odyssey then led me to explore the world of Blender.

Upon entering the upper school level, where I actively enrolled in a computer science course, and after forging a connection with a fellow IT enthusiast at the gym, I reconsidered my initial aspiration of becoming a soldier and since then, I have been resolute in my pursuit of a career in information technology.

In hindsight, I regard the idea of becoming a soldier as rather juvenile. My justification at the time was that it would be considered "cool." I have immense respect and admiration for those who choose to enlist in the armed forces. However, I came to realize that my true calling lay in the field of information technology, a passion that has always been deeply ingrained within me.

I never owned a PlayStation or Xbox, as I was always engrossed in PC activities. It all began with configuring a Minecraft server, in essence. Since upper school, I have fervently delved into the realm of computer science. Initially, I absorbed a wealth of knowledge through numerous tutorials on platforms like YouTube, covering subjects such as Flutter and Python.

Subsequently, I stumbled upon CS50, and my contact at the gym recommended this course, as well as others. I am now gradually approaching the conclusion of CS50 and plan to tackle the CS50 courses "Introduction to Programming with Python" and "AI with Python" thereafter. My hope is to successfully complete my high school diploma and pursue a dual degree program in computer science.

I consciously opt for the dual degree path because I am most effective when learning through hands-on experience, finding practical applications to be much more engaging than theoretical concepts. I believe that this approach will yield a more comprehensive understanding of the subject matter.
