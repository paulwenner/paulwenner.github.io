Me as a computer scientist

I am a German student and would like to become a computer scientist

This website has been created as a result of taking the CS50 course. This website is part of "Problem Set 8" out of 10 (there are 11 problem sets, but counting starts from 0). Once I complete all 11 problem sets, there awaits a final project for me. I expect to have CS50 completed by the end of 2023. Following that, I would like to obtain two more CS50 certificates or take the courses (Introduction to Programming with Python, Introduction to Artificial Intelligence with Python) before my high school graduation in the spring of 2025. I feel that CS50 is significantly advancing my skills, and I have a particular interest in these two subjects.

I am taking CS50 for several reasons. I believe that I can learn very well when I have a structured curriculum and a large community to support me. Additionally, I want to demonstrate my passion for computer science to future employers. My major subjects (three self-selected subjects that give a focus to my high school diploma) are History, Latin, and Ancient Greek.

Especially in Latin and Ancient Greek, it's often necessary to establish connections, and alongside CS50, I want to showcase my enthusiasm and capabilities in my application for a dual degree program. As a basic course, I naturally have Mathematics and Computer Science, but I want to use CS50 to show how valuable this investment is to a company in a dual degree program.

Apart from this, my interests lie in Artificial Intelligence, Blockchain, and Data Science. I find it fascinating to explore and solve problems with algorithms. The concept of Bitcoin continually captivates me, which is undoubtedly the reason why I have invested so much in cryptocurrency. However, the blockchain offers more possibilities than just cryptocurrencies (e.g., Supply Chain Management). I plan to further educate myself in this field and definitely work with blockchain in the future.

My favorite chapter in CS50 so far has been "Week 5 Data Structures in C" because the node data type in C has fascinated me.
